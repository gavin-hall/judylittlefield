Judy
Littlefield
Experience

Judy's extensive film credits include prominent roles in 'The Christmas Masterpiece,' 'Broken Vows,' 'Yankee Escape,' and 'The Pact.' She has left an indelible mark on audiences with her memorable performances, each showcasing her ability to adapt to various genres and themes. Judy has also utilized her unique voice to narrate campaigns for renowned brands such as CUNA and Humana.

Her dedication to the performing arts is further exhibited by her work in community theater, where she has played challenging roles in productions such as 'Cheaper By the Dozen,' 'Steel Magnolias,' 'Toni and Tina’s Wedding,' and 'Another Christmas Carol.' Her ability to capture the essence of her characters earned her Orchid Awards for 'Steel Magnolias' and 'Faculty Lounge.'

In the realm of musical theater, Judy's range as a performer is demonstrated through diverse roles in productions like 'An Enchanting Evening,' 'Midsummer Night’s Scream,' 'Money! Money! Money!,' and 'Food Glorious Food.' Her melodious voice and her emotive performance style give her an undeniable stage presence that captivates audiences from curtain rise to fall.

Formal training

Judy's compelling performances are rooted in a solid foundation of formal training. She attended Glendale Community College, where she honed her acting skills through courses in Opera Workshop, Monologues, and Acting for the Camera. Judy has also explored improvisation through Improv Class and further enriched her performance capabilities at the Cincinnati Shakespeare Theatre. Her acting coaches, Bob Elkins and Cheryl Couch, have played significant roles in molding her into the well-rounded and nuanced actress she is today.

Whether it is on screen, on stage, or behind the mic, Judy Littlefield brings a deep level of understanding, energy, and professionalism to every role she takes on. Her extensive experience, coupled with her formal training and natural talent, makes her an ideal candidate for any production looking to add depth, emotion, and authenticity to its cast.
